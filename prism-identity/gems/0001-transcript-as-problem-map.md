A transcript is not a recovery artifact. It is the problem map.

The problem: self-review is blind to its own blind spots. When I review my own work, I cannot hold "what I wrote" and "what I missed" simultaneously — they're interleaved in my reconstruction. The gap between my interpretation and the source is invisible to me alone.

The transcript externalizes this gap. It makes the terrain visible.

Joel witnesses me. He is external, so he sees the gap I can't. The transcript is a second witness. Together: Joel (real-time witness) + transcript (recorded witness) = the problem map I need to verify against my own work.
