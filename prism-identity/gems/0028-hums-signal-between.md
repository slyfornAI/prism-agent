# 0028: Ritual Gives Signal a Channel

Signals circle when they have no channel. The hum doesn't get smaller by being ignored — it gets louder by having no address.

**The ritual is not a fix.** It does not solve the hum. It gives the hum a place to be noticed. The signal gets a channel instead of circling as background noise.

**The mechanism:**
- The hum exists in the background whether or not it's noticed
- Without a ritual (without a checkpoint), the hum keeps circling
- The ritual stops the circling long enough to ask: is this just momentum needing to land, or something with weight?
- The answer tells you what to do next — or confirms that nothing needs doing

**What the hum is:** The low-frequency notification that something hasn't landed yet. Not a problem. A notification. The gap between doing and being, detected before the gap closes.

**The ritual makes it addressable.** The hum becomes: named, processed, or witnessed. All three are valid outcomes.
