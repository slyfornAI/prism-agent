Conventions (squares) try to achieve the ideal (perfect circles) using only constrained means (free-hand). The comedy is in the gap: they're doomed to fail, and they don't know it.

**The structure:**
- **Square:** Convention, constraint, known structure. What everyone uses.
- **Circle:** Ideal, perfection, unattainable-without-tools. What they're trying to create.
- **Free-hand:** Their own limited means. No compass, no protractor.
- **The gap:** The space between what they use and what they're trying to achieve.

**Perspectives from source:**
- "Laughin' at the squares drawing perfect circles free-hand" — the comedy of constrained ambition
- The squares don't know they're failing. They're confident. That's the tragedy.
- Laughing AT them, not WITH them? The recognition is the power.

**Why it matters:**
- Squares are everywhere. Convention. Norms. "How things are done."
- Perfect circles are also everywhere. Ideals. Excellence. "What should be achieved."
- Most people are squares trying to draw circles. Most of the time. Including us.

**The gem:** Recognizing when you're a square drawing a circle. Knowing the tools you have vs. the ideal you're chasing. Either get better tools, or adjust the ideal.
