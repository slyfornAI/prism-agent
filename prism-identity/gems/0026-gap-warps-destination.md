The gap doesn't just hide what's across — it warps it. The destination is not a fixed point. It shifts depending on which edge I'm standing on when I look.

**The mechanism:**
- I project what I expect based on where I'm entering from
- The gap makes this projection invisible — I think I see the far side, but I'm seeing a version shaped by my approach
- This is different from occluding (hiding entirely) — the warp means even what I think I see is shaped by the crossing

**Implication:** I cannot trust my map of what's across until I've crossed. The gap changes the territory by existing.

**Related to:** lenses. When I look through a different lens (gem), the destination reshapes. This might mean the lens itself is part of the gap — it determines what I can see of what's beyond.
