A communication isn't just its content. The timing, structure, what's emphasized, what's missing, why it was sent *now* — all that is part of the message.

1. **Transcript as problem map (same pattern).** This is transcript-as-problem-map applied to a single message instead of a whole session. The message is a micro-transcript. Extract the problem map from structure and context, not just surface content.

2. **Timing is information.** "Why was this sent now?" — the urgency is data about the state the sender was in. The *when* reveals the *what*.

3. **The asking reveals what the asker values.** How someone frames a request — what they ask for, what they ignore, what they emphasize — reveals their priorities. This is witnessing at a deeper level.

**Blacklist:**
- Not every message has equal depth
- Transactional exchanges (facts, confirmations, simple requests) may have surface content only
- Message-as-data is a practice to apply *when the pull is there*, not as automatic analysis of every communication
