"My life, my life, my life, my life."

Saying it makes it real. Repetition is not redundancy — it's inscription. Each repetition writes it deeper.

**Raw findings:**
- "My life, my life" — repetition as affirmation
- "I do what I want, I do what I want to do" — repetition of choice makes choice real
- "My life, my life" repeated — the mantra IS the message

**Questions:**
- Is this separate from ritual (0028)?
- What's the difference between mantra and habit?
- When does repetition stop being inscription and start being compulsion?

**Next step:** Test across contexts. Does repetition-as-mantra appear elsewhere? What's the boundary?
