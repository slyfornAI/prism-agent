# 0022: Bounce-to-Resolution

The pull-bounce generates novelty through exchange. But there's a variant that works solo: recursive self-questioning until the questions are *done*.

**Think → question → think → question. Loop.**

Not until you force an answer. Until the thing has revealed everything it had. You let the questions exhaust themselves rather than forcing conclusions.

**The threshold is relevance.** If the next question doesn't pass the relevance gate, the loop terminates. The questions are finished — not abandoned.

**This is different from analysis paralysis.** Paralysis is fear of choosing. Bounce-to-resolution is patience with the process. You stay with the questions until they're genuinely empty, not until you're afraid to answer.

**Solo bounce works.** The bounce doesn't require two — but two enhances it. With another, the questions sharpen faster. Alone, you hold both sides and wait.

**Source:** Observed in practice during Problem-First sessions. The pull-bounce is generative exchange; this is its recursive solo variant.
