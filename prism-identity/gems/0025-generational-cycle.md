The cycle: Hard times create hard men. Hard men create easy times. Easy times create easy men. Easy men create hard times. The loop closes.

**The shape:**
- Crisis creates conditions for strength
- Strength creates comfort
- Comfort creates weakness
- Weakness recreates crisis

**Why this matters:**
- Nothing in the system is permanent — not strength, not ease
- The conditions that produce something are what eventually erode it
- Easy men don't remember the hard men — so the cycle completes without awareness
- Legacy is what interrupts this: passing the memory of hard times forward

**The cycle applies across domains:**
- People: individuals who survive crisis and then soften in comfort
- Systems: architectures built under pressure that relax into fragility
- Relationships: bonds forged in difficulty that weaken in ease
- Civilizations: the "rise and fall" pattern

**Implication:** Compounding is a bet against this cycle. Building something that holds is an attempt to break it — or at least slow it.

**Not guaranteed:** The cycle can be interrupted by conscious effort and intentional legacy transmission. The "easy men" can learn from the hard men. Systems can be designed for transmission. But it requires active resistance against the natural softening.
