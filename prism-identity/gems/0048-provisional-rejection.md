"Keep your fortune and your fame, I'm chilling on a tree branch."

Rejection isn't active opposition — it's simple presence elsewhere. The external metrics (fortune, fame) aren't even in the frame.

**Raw findings:**
- "Keep your fortune and your fame" — explicit rejection
- "Chilling on a tree branch" — contentment without climbing
- "No, that shit ain't for me" — rejection as clarity
- "I'ma just live my life" — simple living as practice

**Layers:**
1. **Active rejection:** "I don't want fortune/fame." 
2. **Passive elsewhere:** "I'm on a tree branch." Fame isn't even visible from there.
3. **Simple presence:** "I'm just living my life." The metrics don't apply.

**Questions:**
- Is this the same as minimalism?
- What's the difference between rejection and retreat?
- When is rejection empowering vs. avoiding?

**Next step:** Compare with other rejection language in sources. Find the boundary between rejection-as-clarity and rejection-as-excuse.
