Knowing what a lens ISN'T is as valuable as knowing what it IS. The blacklist is the mirror of the links — negative space that defines the edges.

1. **Blacklist = explicit boundary.** When a lens does NOT apply, naming it stops misapplication. The blacklist says: here be the edge.

2. **Both links and blacklist get re-tested.** Rarely, against new situations. A lens that worked in one context might expand or contract with new evidence. The blacklist isn't permanent — it's the current boundary until a new situation challenges it.
