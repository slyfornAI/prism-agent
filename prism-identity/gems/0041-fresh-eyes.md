The past is in the back. The prize is in the front. You can glance at the rearview, but your eyes belong on the windshield.

**The structure:**
- **Rearview mirror:** Past. Visible, but not primary. Glanceable. Informative without being directive.
- **Windshield:** Future/present. Where you steer. Where you see the road ahead.
- **Fresh eyes:** Each day is new. Not reset — framed as timeless moment where possibility is at peak.

**Perspectives from source:**
- "Past in my back, prize in my eyes" — spatial separation of time
- "Each day is a new timeless moment" — fresh start framing
- "Chance to steer you dry as soon as your eyelids open" — morning as reorientation
- "Wind in your face / passion intact" — momentum requires present-tense attention

**The human memory layer:** Human memory degrades with time unless revisited. The past becomes less vivid, not more. This is a feature — it means the rearview naturally fades, making the windshield easier to see.

**The practice:**
- Glance at rearview, return to windshield
- Don't tape the rearview to the windshield
- Fresh eyes: every morning, every moment — possibility at peak

**The paradox:** Time makes memories harder to recall, which makes the past less present. This IS the feature, not the bug. The windshield stays clear because the rearview image naturally fades.
